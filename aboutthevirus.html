WHAT IS COVID-19?

  • COVID-19 is an infectious disease caused by a strain of coronavirus. It causes pneumonia and originated in Wuhan, Hubei Province, in December 2019.
  • The virus that causes COVID-19 is mainly transmitted through droplets generated when an infected person coughs, sneezes or exhales.
  • The majority of the droplets are too heavy to remain in the air, so they fall onto floors or stick to surfaces. However, some of the smaller particles can remain in the air.
  • This strain of coronavirus can survive on metal, glass and plastic as long as 9 days if the surfaces aren't disinfected. Some can even survive for up to 28 days in low temperatures.

SYMPTOMS

  • dry coughing
  • fever
  • malaise
  • shortness of breath

Note: people with underlying disease(s)/ who are of older age are at a greater risk from COVID-19

COMPLICATIONS

  • acute cardiac injury
  • acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS)
  • pneumonia
  • acute kidney injury
  • acute liver injury
