MISCONCEPTIONS

1. Microwaving your mail kills COVID-19: FALSE

There have been no cases of anybody getting the virus from the mail as of May 2020. Before your mail ever reaches your house, it goes through several days of delivery and processing. By the time the mail arrives at your doorstep, the virus will have died already. Furthermore, microwaves were built to heat up food and beverages by interacting with the water molecules within, and simply won't heat up something like paper. Lastly, there are no set standards for using microwaves to disinfect objects.

2. Antibodies in breastmilk can prevent/ cure COVID-19 in adults: FALSE

While some people claim that breast milk has immune-boosting properties, many doctors have debunked this myth, stating that drinking breast milk provides no health benefits to adults and may even lead to various illnesses. Similarly, breast milk does not contain COVID-19 antibodies. Dr. Dyan Hes, a pediatrician with NY City's Gramercy Pediatrics, stated that "There's no proof that breast milk at all can cure COVID or give you antibodies".

3. COVID-19 is just as deadly/ less deadly than the flu: FALSE

While COVID-19 is less deadly than SARS, disease experts have agreed that the virus is significantly more lethal than seasonal flu. Research so far indicates that COVID-19 spreads more easily and has a higher death rate than the flu. The death rate from seasonal flu is around 0.1% in the US, while nearly 6% have died among reported COVID-19 cases in the US as of May 2020. Researchers from Columbia University recently estimated that only 1 in 12 cases of COVID-19 in the U.S. are documented, which they said would translate to an infection fatality rate of about 0.6%, according to The Washington Post. Furthermore, unlike the flu, there is currently no vaccine for COVID-19, which theoretically makes everyone susceptible to it. According to the CDC, the flu affects 8% of the US population every year. In comparison, a study published March 30 in the journal 'The Lancet' estimates that between 50% and 80% of the population could be infected with COVID-19.

4. Miracle Mineral Solution (MMS) can cure COVID-19: FALSE

Miracle Mineral Solution does not cure COVID-19 and has not been approved by the FDA for any use. The solution, when mixed as directed, forms industrial bleach that may cause serious and potentially life-threatening side effects.
