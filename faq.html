Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Should I be tested for COVID-19?

A: Not everyone needs to be tested for COVID-19. Even if you are mildly ill, you might not need to be tested and can most likely recover at home without medical care. However, if you are exhibiting symptoms of COVID-19 and want to be tested, contact your healthcare provider first.

Q: Should I cancel my overseas trip?

A: It is recommended that you avoid all nonessential trips overseas. Many countries have implemented travel restrictions and mandatory quaratines, and Airlines have cancelled many international flights as well. If you need to travel, it is advised that you avoid contact with sick people, avoid touching your eyes, nose, or mouth with unwashed hands, and wash your hands regularly, for at least 20 seconds with soap. If soap and water is unavailable to you, use hand sanitizers that contain at least 60% alcohol.

Q: What is the source of COVID-19?

A: COVID-19 originates from a coronavirus called SARS-CoV-2. Coronaviruses are a large family of viruses that are commonly found in people among different of animals, including camels, cats, and bats. In rare cases, animal coronaviruses infect people and cause it to spread between others, which is precisely what happened with COVID-19.

Q: What should I do if someone in my house gets sick?

A: If possible, use a separate room and bathroom for the sick household member. Ensure that the sick household member is wearing a facemask at home to prevent spreading the disease to others. Try to avoid contact with the sick person and avoid sharing utensils, food and drinks. If possible, clean and disinfect the house regularly, especially objects that are often touched or used.

Q: How can I prevent COVID-19?

A: The best way to prevent COVID-19 is to get vaccinated with an FDA-approved or FDA-authorized COVID-19 vaccine and stay up to date on your COVID-19 vaccines.
